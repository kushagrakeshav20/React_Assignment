Mountain and ocean

There's a feeling of silent boredom that one receives from the sight of the beach. The sand ripples in the visage of desolation, teems with dull undulating waves slapping the sand with a bored "smack" without an echo. However, the mountains are alive. They roll in a perpetual explosion of color and movement that floods the eyes and ears, fills the nose with soft aroma and the mouth with sweet oxygen. The mountains are an intense arrangement of vivid color and form unmatched by any other type of geology. The beach huddles in a mass of ugliness beside it.

Sunset and Birds

The surrounding area is desolate, filled with cacti, brush, and the semi-frequent jackrabbit that is there to remind you of the surrounding animals whose kingdom you have called home for the night. This may be their world but together we share the same experience of the daily rituals of the sky. The phases of sunrise and sunset, where the sun gets replaced by the moon — the constant to remind us of the sacredness of life.

Sunset and Boat

Have you ever experienced a night sky that pierced your heart and landed deep in your memory? I have. One night in August 2016, I had a powerful night sky moment, which made me think about the amazing skies I have seen. Of course, there have been some not so nice skies, foretelling bad weather — from hurricanes and tornadoes to sleet and blizzards. And I have enjoyed cloudless sunrises glowing over several oceans, sparkling the vast dancing waters, and stretching out to embrace the beach and me; I can never get enough of those moments. But that’s not what I thought of that August night. My mind focused on sunsets.
